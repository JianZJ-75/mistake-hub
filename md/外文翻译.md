## 英文原文

### Enhancing Human Learning via Spaced Repetition Optimization

**Behzad Tabibian, Utkarsh Upadhyay, Abir De, Ali Zarezade, Bernhard Schölkopf, Manuel Gomez-Rodriguez**

Max Planck Institute for Software Systems; Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems

---

#### Significance

Early memory research relied on small-scale controlled experiments to characterize human memory. These experiments revealed several key findings—for example, that the frequency of reviews, the temporal spacing between reviews, and the time elapsed since the last review all significantly affect memory retention. However, despite providing a valuable theoretical foundation for learning strategy design, current spaced repetition tools remain rule-based heuristics with hard-coded parameters that do not leverage the automated fine-grained monitoring and greater degree of control offered by modern online learning platforms. This work introduces a stochastic optimal control framework for designing spaced repetition algorithms with provable optimality guarantees that can adapt to individual learner differences. We validate the proposed algorithm on large-scale data from Duolingo, demonstrating significant advantages over existing heuristic methods.

---

#### Abstract

Spaced repetition is a technique for efficient memorization which uses repeated review of content following a schedule determined by a spaced repetition algorithm to improve long-term retention. However, current spaced repetition algorithms are simple rule-based heuristics with a few hard-coded parameters, which do not leverage the automated fine-grained monitoring and greater degree of control offered by modern online learning platforms. In this paper, we introduce a flexible representation of spaced repetition using the framework of marked temporal point processes and then address the design of spaced repetition algorithms with provable guarantees as an optimal control problem for stochastic differential equations with jumps. For two well-known human memory models, we prove the following: if the learner aims to maximize the recall probability subject to a cost on reviewing frequency, the optimal reviewing schedule is given by the recall probability itself. Based on this theoretical result, we develop a simple, scalable online spaced repetition algorithm, MEMORIZE, to determine the optimal reviewing times. Finally, we validate the proposed algorithm using large-scale natural experiment data gathered from Duolingo, a popular language-learning online platform.

---

#### 1 Introduction

In 1885, the German psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus first systematically demonstrated through a series of pioneering self-experiments that memory retention is closely related to the frequency of reviews, the temporal spacing between reviews, and the time elapsed since the last review. His research revealed the famous "forgetting curve"—memory decays exponentially after learning, but this process can be effectively slowed through appropriately scheduled reviews. In the more than a century since, numerous psychologists and cognitive scientists have built upon his foundational work to investigate the mechanisms of human memory in depth, proposing various memory models and theoretical frameworks.

Spaced repetition is a learning technique developed based on these memory research findings. Its core idea is to maximize long-term memory retention by appropriately scheduling review intervals—reviewing a knowledge item just as the learner is about to forget it. This method shows significant advantages in long-term memory retention compared to massed practice (i.e., "cramming"), a phenomenon known as the spacing effect.

Several spaced repetition systems have been developed and widely adopted. The most representative include the Leitner system, which uses graded card boxes to dynamically adjust review intervals based on how well the learner has mastered each knowledge item; and the SuperMemo family of algorithms (such as the SM-2 algorithm), which introduces an "easiness factor" to adaptively adjust subsequent review intervals based on the learner's recall performance. These methods have achieved remarkable results in practice, driving the development of well-known learning tools such as Anki, Quizlet, and Duolingo.

However, existing spaced repetition algorithms have a fundamental limitation: they are essentially rule-based heuristics that lack solid theoretical optimality guarantees. For example, Reddy et al. (2016) proposed a queueing-theory-based analytical approach for the Leitner system and developed a heuristic approximation algorithm. However, their heuristic approximation does not have provable performance guarantees and does not adapt over time. Moreover, existing methods typically rely on a small number of hard-coded parameters and cannot fully leverage the rich user behavior data provided by modern online learning platforms.

The core contribution of this paper is to rigorously formulate the design of spaced repetition algorithms as an optimal control problem. Specifically, we: (1) use the marked temporal point process framework to formally model the spaced repetition process; (2) formulate the optimal review scheduling problem as a stochastic optimal control problem for stochastic differential equations with jumps; (3) derive the optimal review strategy in closed analytical form; (4) propose the MEMORIZE algorithm as a practical implementation of the optimal strategy; and (5) validate the algorithm's effectiveness using large-scale real-world data.

#### 2 Modeling Framework

##### 2.1 Memory Model

We adopt an exponential forgetting model to describe a learner's memory state for a specific learning item. For learning item *i*, the recall probability at time *t* is modeled as:

$$m_i(t) := P(recall) = \exp[-n_i(t) \cdot (t - t_r)]$$

where $n_i(t)$ is the forgetting rate and $t_r$ is the time of the last review. This model derives directly from the classical mathematical expression of the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, with the core assumption that between any two reviews, the recall probability decays exponentially, with the decay rate determined by the forgetting rate parameter.

##### 2.2 Review Event Modeling

Each review event is represented as a triplet (learning item, review time, recall outcome), where the recall outcome is a binary variable: successful recall (r = 1) or recall failure (r = 0). We use marked temporal point processes to model the arrival process of review events, where the mark of each event corresponds to the recall outcome.

##### 2.3 Dynamics of the Forgetting Rate

Based on the Half-Life Regression model, the effect of recall outcomes on the forgetting rate is modeled as follows:

- **Successful recall**: the forgetting rate is multiplied by a decay factor $(1 - \alpha)$, i.e., $n \leftarrow n \cdot (1 - \alpha)$
- **Recall failure**: the forgetting rate is multiplied by a growth factor $(1 + \beta)$, i.e., $n \leftarrow n \cdot (1 + \beta)$

where $\alpha \in (0, 1)$ and $\beta > 0$ are model parameters. The intuition behind this mechanism is that successful recall strengthens the memory trace, reducing the forgetting rate; while recall failure indicates a weaker memory trace, so the forgetting rate should increase.

##### 2.4 Memory Dynamics Equation

Based on the above modeling, we derive the stochastic differential equation for the recall probability (Proposition 1):

$$dm_i(t) = -n_i(t) \cdot m_i(t) \, dt + (1 - m_i(t)) \, dN_i(t)$$

This equation characterizes the continuous-time dynamics of the recall probability: the first term $-n_i(t) \cdot m_i(t) \, dt$ describes the natural decay of memory; the second term $(1 - m_i(t)) \, dN_i(t)$ describes the instantaneous boost to memory from a review event—a review causes the recall probability to jump from its current value $m_i(t)$ to 1 (complete recall), so the magnitude of the boost is exactly $1 - m_i(t)$.

---

#### 3 Optimal Review Scheduling

##### 3.1 Optimization Objective

We formulate the optimal review scheduling for spaced repetition as the following stochastic optimal control problem: the learner's goal is to minimize the expected total loss function:

$$\min_{u(\cdot)} \; \mathbb{E}\left[\phi(m(t_f), n(t_f)) + \int_0^{t_f} \ell(m(\tau), n(\tau), u(\tau)) \, d\tau\right]$$

where $u(t)$ is the reviewing intensity (i.e., the review frequency per unit time), $t_f$ is the end of the learning period, $\phi$ is the terminal loss function, and $\ell$ is the instantaneous loss function. The instantaneous loss function penalizes both forgetting (low recall probability) and excessive review frequency (review cost), thereby achieving a balance between learning effectiveness and review efficiency.

##### 3.2 Optimal Reviewing Intensity

By solving the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation, we obtain the analytical expression for the optimal reviewing intensity of a single learning item (Theorem 3):

$$u^*(t) = q^{-1/2} \cdot (1 - m(t))$$

where $q$ is the review cost parameter. This result has an elegant intuitive interpretation: **the optimal reviewing intensity is proportional to the forgetting probability**. When the learner is about to forget a knowledge item (i.e., $m(t)$ is low and $1 - m(t)$ is high), the algorithm schedules more frequent reviews; when the item is well remembered ($m(t)$ is close to 1), the review frequency naturally decreases. This result mathematically validates the core intuition of spaced repetition: reviewing is most effective when the learner is on the verge of forgetting.

For the case of multiple learning items (Theorem 4), the optimal reviewing intensity can be computed independently for each item:

$$u_i^*(t) = q_i^{-1/2} \cdot (1 - m_i(t))$$

This means that multi-item scheduling can be decomposed into independent single-item scheduling problems, greatly simplifying the practical implementation of the algorithm.

---

#### 4 The MEMORIZE Algorithm

Based on the above theoretical results, we propose the MEMORIZE algorithm—a simple, scalable online spaced repetition scheduling algorithm. The core procedure of the MEMORIZE algorithm is as follows:

1. **Initialization**: For each learning item *i*, set the initial forgetting rate $n_i(0)$ and the initial recall probability $m_i(0) = 1$.
2. **Sampling review times**: Based on the current optimal reviewing intensity $u_i^*(t)$, sample the next review time via an inhomogeneous Poisson process. The reviewing intensity increases as memory decays, ensuring that a review is triggered as the learner is about to forget.
3. **Execute review and update**: After a review occurs, update the forgetting rate $n_i$ according to the recall outcome (success or failure), and reset the recall probability to 1.
4. **Repeat**: Return to step 2 and continue scheduling.

The key advantages of the MEMORIZE algorithm are: (1) it has provable optimality guarantees, rather than relying on empirical heuristic rules; (2) it can adaptively adjust the review schedule based on each learner's actual performance; (3) the algorithm can run independently in parallel across multiple learning items, offering good scalability; and (4) the algorithm implementation is simple, requiring only the maintenance of each item's forgetting rate and last review time.

#### 5 Experimental Validation

##### 5.1 Dataset

We used large-scale learning behavior data collected from Duolingo—one of the world's most popular online language learning platforms—to validate the proposed algorithm. The dataset contains approximately 12 million learning sessions, involving approximately 5.3 million unique (user, word) pairs, spanning a two-week period. After filtering (requiring at least 30 review records per user-word pair), the final dataset contains approximately 5.2 million (user, word) pairs.

##### 5.2 Experimental Design

Since directly controlling the review behavior of Duolingo users is neither practical nor ethical, we adopted a natural experiment design. The core observation is that users frequently deviate from Duolingo's recommended review times in actual use, choosing on their own which content to review and when to review it. This naturally occurring behavioral deviation allows us to assign users to different "treatment groups" based on how closely their actual review patterns match the predicted patterns of each algorithm.

Specifically, for each (user, word) pair, we compute the likelihood of its actual review time series under three different scheduling strategies: (1) uniform scheduling—reviewing at a constant rate $\mu$; (2) threshold-based scheduling—triggering a review when the recall probability drops below a certain threshold; and (3) MEMORIZE scheduling—reviewing according to the optimal strategy proposed in this paper. The (user, word) pair is then assigned to the group with the highest likelihood.

##### 5.3 Evaluation Metric

To evaluate learning effectiveness in a model-agnostic manner, we defined the empirical forgetting rate as the core evaluation metric:

$$\hat{n} = -\frac{\log(\hat{m}(t_n))}{t_n - t_{n-1}}$$

where $\hat{m}(t_n)$ is the estimated recall probability from the last review session, and $t_n$ and $t_{n-1}$ are the times of the last and second-to-last reviews, respectively. **A lower empirical forgetting rate indicates more effective learning**—the learner maintained a higher level of memory retention at the end of the review interval.

##### 5.4 Experimental Results

The experimental results strongly validated the effectiveness of the MEMORIZE algorithm:

**(1) Between-group comparison:** The user group whose review patterns followed the MEMORIZE strategy exhibited significantly lower empirical forgetting rates than the groups following the uniform scheduling strategy and the threshold-based scheduling strategy. This difference was statistically significant (Mann-Whitney U test, $p < 0.05$).

**(2) Training period effect:** The advantage of the MEMORIZE algorithm grew with longer training periods. Over shorter training periods, the differences among the three strategies were relatively small; however, as the training period increased, the reduction in forgetting rate achieved by MEMORIZE became increasingly pronounced. This indicates that the advantage of optimal review scheduling requires time to accumulate and manifest.

**(3) Individual-level analysis:** In an individual-level analysis of 322 learners, we found a significant negative correlation between the degree to which learners adhered to the MEMORIZE strategy (measured by log-likelihood) and their empirical forgetting rate (Pearson correlation analysis). In other words, the more closely a learner followed the MEMORIZE strategy's recommended review times, the lower their forgetting rate and the better their learning outcomes.

**(4) Ruling out selection bias:** To rule out potential confounding factors (e.g., users in different groups might happen to be studying words of different difficulty levels), we conducted a thorough selection bias analysis in the supplementary materials. The results confirmed that the observed differences in effectiveness were indeed due to differences in review scheduling strategies, rather than differences in learning material difficulty.

---

#### 6 Discussion

This paper proposes a framework for designing spaced repetition algorithms based on stochastic optimal control theory. Unlike previous approaches based on empirical rules, our method has a solid mathematical theoretical foundation, and the resulting optimal review strategy has an elegant analytical form: the optimal reviewing intensity is proportional to the forgetting probability. Based on this theoretical result, we developed the MEMORIZE algorithm and validated its effectiveness using large-scale real-world data from Duolingo.

Our work opens up several important future research directions:

**Large-scale interventional experiments**: The experimental validation in this paper is based on a natural experiment design, leveraging users' spontaneous deviations from recommended review times. Future work could collaborate with platforms such as Duolingo to conduct large-scale randomized controlled experiments, directly randomly assigning users to different scheduling strategy groups, thereby enabling stronger causal inference.

**Hard constraints on review counts**: The current optimization objective "softly" limits review frequency through a cost term in the loss function. In practice, learners have limited available study time each day, so introducing hard constraints on review counts would be a more realistic modeling approach.

**Dependencies between learning items**: The current model assumes that the memory processes for different learning items are independent. However, in actual learning, reviewing one word may simultaneously strengthen other related words (e.g., words sharing the same root). Modeling such inter-item dependencies would further improve scheduling effectiveness.

**Validation over longer time spans**: The current dataset covers only two weeks of learning behavior. Validating algorithm effectiveness over longer time spans will provide deeper insights into the long-term mechanisms of spaced repetition.

**Broader application domains**: The stochastic optimal control framework for stochastic differential equations with jumps is applicable not only to spaced repetition but may also play a role in other learning and behavioral optimization problems involving pulsed interventions. For example, in medication adherence management, health behavior interventions, and personalized recommendation systems, similar modeling approaches all have potential application value.

---

#### 7 Conclusion

This paper reexamines the design of spaced repetition algorithms from the perspective of stochastic optimal control. Our core finding is that, under the exponential forgetting memory model, the optimal review scheduling intensity is proportional to the learner's forgetting probability—a result that is not only mathematically elegant but also perfectly consistent with the core intuition of spaced repetition. The MEMORIZE algorithm developed based on this theoretical result demonstrated significant advantages over traditional heuristic methods on a large-scale dataset of 12 million learning sessions from Duolingo. We hope that this work will provide a solid theoretical foundation and practical guidance for designing more efficient and personalized intelligent learning systems.

#### 摘要

间隔重复是一种高效的记忆技术，它通过按照由间隔重复算法确定的调度计划反复复习学习内容，以提高长期记忆保持率。然而，当前的间隔重复算法本质上都是简单的、基于规则的启发式方法，仅包含少量硬编码参数，无法有效利用现代在线学习平台所提供的自动化细粒度监控和更高程度的控制能力。在本文中，我们使用标记时间点过程（marked temporal point processes）的框架，引入了一种灵活的间隔重复表示方法，并将间隔重复算法的设计建模为带跳跃的随机微分方程（stochastic differential equations with jumps）的最优控制问题。对于两种广为人知的人类记忆模型，我们证明了以下结论：当学习者的目标是在受限的复习频率成本下最大化记忆召回概率时，最优的复习调度方案由记忆召回概率本身所决定。基于这一理论结果，我们开发了一种简洁、可扩展的在线间隔重复算法——MEMORIZE，用于确定最优复习时间。最后，我们通过来自 Duolingo（一个广受欢迎的语言学习在线平台）的大规模自然实验数据，对所提出的算法进行了验证。

#### 1 引言

1885 年，德国心理学家赫尔曼·艾宾浩斯（Hermann Ebbinghaus）通过一系列开创性的自我实验首次系统地证明：记忆的保持与复习的频率、复习的时间间隔以及距离上次复习所经过的时间密切相关。他的研究揭示了著名的"遗忘曲线"——记忆在学习后呈指数级衰减，但通过合理安排的复习可以有效延缓这一过程。此后的一个多世纪里，众多心理学家和认知科学家在其基础上深入研究了人类记忆的机制，提出了多种记忆模型和理论框架。

间隔重复（Spaced Repetition）正是基于上述记忆研究成果发展而来的学习技术。其核心思想在于：通过合理安排复习的时间间隔——在学习者即将遗忘某一知识点时恰好进行复习——来最大化长期记忆保持效果。这一方法相较于集中式学习（即"突击复习"或"填鸭式学习"），在长期记忆保持方面具有显著优势，这一现象被称为间距效应（spacing effect）。

目前已有多种间隔重复系统被开发并广泛应用。其中最具代表性的包括：莱特纳系统（Leitner system），它使用分级卡片盒来根据学习者对每个知识点的掌握程度动态调整复习间隔；以及 SuperMemo 系列算法（如 SM-2 算法），它通过引入"易度因子"（easiness factor）来根据学习者的回忆表现自适应地调整后续复习间隔。这些方法在实践中取得了令人瞩目的效果，推动了 Anki、Quizlet、Duolingo 等知名学习工具的发展。

然而，现有的间隔重复算法存在一个根本性的局限：它们本质上都是基于规则的启发式方法，缺乏坚实的理论最优性保证。例如，Reddy 等人于 2016 年提出了一种基于排队论模型的莱特纳系统分析方法，并开发了一种启发式近似算法。然而，该方法的启发式近似并不具备可证明的性能保证，且未能随时间推移进行自适应调整。此外，现有方法通常依赖于少量硬编码参数，无法充分利用现代在线学习平台所提供的丰富用户行为数据。

本文的核心贡献在于将间隔重复算法的设计严格建模为一个最优控制问题。具体而言，我们：（1）使用标记时间点过程框架对间隔重复过程进行形式化建模；（2）将最优复习调度问题表述为带跳跃的随机微分方程的随机最优控制问题；（3）推导出解析形式的最优复习策略；（4）提出 MEMORIZE 算法作为最优策略的实际实现；（5）通过大规模真实数据验证了算法的有效性。

#### 2 建模框架

##### 2.1 记忆模型

我们采用指数遗忘模型来描述学习者对特定学习项目的记忆状态。对于学习项目 *i*，在时刻 *t* 的记忆召回概率建模为：

$$m_i(t) := P(recall) = \exp[-n_i(t) \cdot (t - t_r)]$$

其中，$n_i(t)$ 为遗忘速率（forgetting rate），$t_r$ 为上次复习时间。该模型直接源于艾宾浩斯遗忘曲线的经典数学表达式，其核心假设是：在两次复习之间，记忆召回概率按指数规律衰减，衰减速率由遗忘速率参数决定。

##### 2.2 复习事件建模

每次复习事件被表示为一个三元组（学习项目、复习时间、回忆结果），其中回忆结果为二值变量：成功回忆（r = 1）或回忆失败（r = 0）。我们使用标记时间点过程来建模复习事件的到达过程，其中每个事件的标记（mark）对应回忆结果。

##### 2.3 遗忘速率的动态更新

基于半衰期回归模型（Half-Life Regression），复习结果对遗忘速率的影响被建模如下：

- **成功回忆**：遗忘速率乘以衰减因子 $(1 - \alpha)$，即 $n \leftarrow n \cdot (1 - \alpha)$
- **回忆失败**：遗忘速率乘以增长因子 $(1 + \beta)$，即 $n \leftarrow n \cdot (1 + \beta)$

其中 $\alpha \in (0, 1)$ 和 $\beta > 0$ 是模型参数。这一机制的直觉是：成功的回忆强化了记忆痕迹，降低了遗忘速率；而回忆失败则表明记忆痕迹较弱，因此遗忘速率应当增加。

##### 2.4 记忆动态方程

基于上述建模，我们推导出记忆召回概率的随机微分方程（命题 1）：

$$dm_i(t) = -n_i(t) \cdot m_i(t) \, dt + (1 - m_i(t)) \, dN_i(t)$$

该方程刻画了记忆召回概率的连续时间动态：第一项 $-n_i(t) \cdot m_i(t) \, dt$ 描述了记忆的自然衰减过程；第二项 $(1 - m_i(t)) \, dN_i(t)$ 描述了复习事件对记忆的即时提升效果——复习使记忆召回概率从当前值 $m_i(t)$ 跳跃至 1（完全记忆），因此提升量恰好为 $1 - m_i(t)$。

#### 3 最优复习调度

##### 3.1 优化目标

我们将间隔重复的最优复习调度建模为如下随机最优控制问题：学习者的目标是最小化期望总损失函数：

$$\min_{u(\cdot)} \; \mathbb{E}\left[\phi(m(t_f), n(t_f)) + \int_0^{t_f} \ell(m(\tau), n(\tau), u(\tau)) \, d\tau\right]$$

其中 $u(t)$ 为复习强度（即单位时间内的复习频率），$t_f$ 为学习周期终点，$\phi$ 为终端损失函数，$\ell$ 为瞬时损失函数。瞬时损失函数同时惩罚遗忘（低召回概率）和过高的复习频率（复习成本），从而在学习效果和复习效率之间取得平衡。

##### 3.2 最优复习强度

通过求解 Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman 方程，我们得到了单个学习项目的最优复习强度的解析表达式（定理 3）：

$$u^*(t) = q^{-1/2} \cdot (1 - m(t))$$

其中 $q$ 为复习成本参数。该结果具有优美的直觉解释：**最优的复习强度与遗忘概率成正比**。当学习者即将遗忘某一知识点时（即 $m(t)$ 较低，$1 - m(t)$ 较高），算法会安排更频繁的复习；而当知识点记忆牢固时（$m(t)$ 接近 1），复习频率自然降低。这一结果从数学上精确验证了间隔重复的核心直觉：在即将遗忘时复习最为有效。

对于多个学习项目的情形（定理 4），最优复习强度可以独立地为每个项目分别计算：

$$u_i^*(t) = q_i^{-1/2} \cdot (1 - m_i(t))$$

这意味着多项目调度可以分解为独立的单项目调度问题，极大地简化了算法的实际实现。

---

#### 4 MEMORIZE 算法

基于上述理论结果，我们提出了 MEMORIZE 算法——一种简洁、可扩展的在线间隔重复调度算法。MEMORIZE 算法的核心流程如下：

1. **初始化**：为每个学习项目 *i* 设定初始遗忘速率 $n_i(0)$ 和初始召回概率 $m_i(0) = 1$。
2. **采样复习时间**：基于当前的最优复习强度 $u_i^*(t)$，通过非齐次泊松过程（inhomogeneous Poisson process）采样下一次复习时间。复习强度随记忆衰减而增加，确保在学习者即将遗忘时触发复习。
3. **执行复习与更新**：在复习发生后，根据回忆结果（成功或失败）更新遗忘速率 $n_i$，并将记忆召回概率重置为 1。
4. **重复**：返回步骤 2，持续进行调度。

MEMORIZE 算法的关键优势在于：（1）它具有可证明的最优性保证，而非基于经验的启发式规则；（2）它能够根据每个学习者的实际表现自适应地调整复习调度；（3）算法对多个学习项目可独立并行运行，具有良好的可扩展性；（4）算法实现简洁，仅需维护每个项目的遗忘速率和上次复习时间。

---

#### 5 实验验证

##### 5.1 数据集

我们使用了从 Duolingo——全球最受欢迎的在线语言学习平台之一——收集的大规模学习行为数据来验证所提出的算法。该数据集包含约 1200 万次学习会话，涉及约 530 万个唯一的（用户，单词）对，时间跨度为两周。经过过滤（每个用户-单词对至少包含 30 次复习记录），最终数据集包含约 520 万个（用户，单词）对。

##### 5.2 实验设计

由于直接控制 Duolingo 用户的复习行为既不现实也不道德，我们采用了自然实验（natural experiment）的设计方法。核心观察是：用户在实际使用中经常偏离 Duolingo 系统推荐的复习时间，自行选择复习哪些内容和何时复习。这种自然发生的行为偏差使得我们可以根据用户实际复习模式与各算法预测模式之间的匹配程度，将用户分配到不同的"处理组"。

具体而言，对于每一个（用户，单词）对，我们计算其实际复习时间序列在三种不同调度策略下的似然度：（1）均匀调度——以恒定速率 $\mu$ 进行复习；（2）阈值调度——当记忆召回概率降至某一阈值以下时触发复习；（3）MEMORIZE 调度——按照本文提出的最优策略进行复习。然后根据似然度最高的策略将该（用户，单词）对分配至相应组别。

##### 5.3 评估指标

为了以模型无关的方式评估学习效果，我们定义了经验遗忘速率作为核心评估指标：

$$\hat{n} = -\frac{\log(\hat{m}(t_n))}{t_n - t_{n-1}}$$

其中 $\hat{m}(t_n)$ 为最后一次复习会话中估计的记忆召回概率，$t_n$ 和 $t_{n-1}$ 分别为最后一次和倒数第二次复习的时间。**较低的经验遗忘速率意味着更有效的学习**——学习者在复习间隔结束时保持了更高的记忆水平。

##### 5.4 实验结果

实验结果有力地验证了 MEMORIZE 算法的有效性：

**（1）组间比较：** 按照 MEMORIZE 策略进行复习的用户组，其经验遗忘速率显著低于按照均匀调度策略和阈值调度策略进行复习的用户组。这一差异在统计上是显著的（Mann-Whitney U 检验，$p < 0.05$）。

**（2）训练周期效应：** MEMORIZE 算法的优势随着训练周期的延长而增大。在较短的训练周期内，三种策略的差异相对较小；但随着训练周期的增加，MEMORIZE 算法带来的遗忘速率降低效果愈加显著。这表明最优复习调度的优势需要一定的时间来积累和显现。

**（3）个体层面分析：** 在对 322 名学习者进行的个体层面分析中，我们发现学习者与 MEMORIZE 策略的一致程度（以对数似然度衡量）与其经验遗忘速率之间存在显著的负相关关系（Pearson 相关分析）。换言之，学习者越严格地遵循 MEMORIZE 策略的推荐复习时间，其遗忘速率越低，学习效果越好。

**（4）排除选择偏差：** 为了排除可能的混淆因素（例如，不同组别的用户可能恰好学习了难度不同的单词），我们在补充材料中进行了详尽的选择偏差分析，结果证实观察到的效果差异确实源于复习调度策略的不同，而非学习材料难度的差异。

---

#### 6 讨论

本文提出了一种基于随机最优控制理论的间隔重复算法设计框架。与此前基于经验规则的方法不同，我们的方法具有坚实的数学理论基础，所得到的最优复习策略具有优美的解析形式：最优复习强度与遗忘概率成正比。基于这一理论结果，我们开发了 MEMORIZE 算法，并通过来自 Duolingo 的大规模真实数据验证了其有效性。

我们的工作开辟了若干重要的未来研究方向：

**大规模干预实验**：本文的实验验证基于自然实验设计，即利用用户自发偏离推荐复习时间的行为。未来可以与 Duolingo 等平台合作，开展大规模随机对照实验，直接将用户随机分配到不同的调度策略组，从而获得更强的因果推断。

**复习次数硬约束**：当前的优化目标通过损失函数中的成本项来"软性"限制复习频率。在实际应用中，学习者每天的可用学习时间是有限的，因此在复习次数上引入硬约束是更加现实的建模方式。

**学习项目间的依赖关系**：当前模型假设各学习项目的记忆过程是独立的。然而在实际学习中，复习一个单词可能会同时强化与之相关的其他单词（如同一词根的词汇）。建模这种项目间依赖关系将进一步提升调度效果。

**更长时间跨度的验证**：当前数据集仅涵盖两周的学习行为。在更长的时间跨度上验证算法效果，将为理解间隔重复的长期机制提供更深入的洞察。

**更广泛的应用领域**：带跳跃的随机微分方程的随机最优控制框架不仅适用于间隔重复，还可能在其他涉及脉冲式干预的学习和行为优化问题中发挥作用。例如，在药物依从性管理、健康行为干预、以及个性化推荐系统等领域，类似的建模思路都具有潜在的应用价值。

---

#### 7 结论

本文从随机最优控制的视角重新审视了间隔重复算法的设计问题。我们的核心发现是：在指数遗忘记忆模型下，最优的复习调度强度与学习者的遗忘概率成正比——这一结果不仅数学上优美，更与间隔重复的核心直觉完全一致。基于这一理论结果开发的 MEMORIZE 算法，在来自 Duolingo 的 1200 万次学习会话的大规模数据集上，展现出了相较于传统启发式方法的显著优势。我们希望本文的工作能够为设计更加高效、个性化的智能学习系统提供坚实的理论基础和实践指导。
